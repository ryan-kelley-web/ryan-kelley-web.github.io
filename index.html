    What I Do

    I love creating better outcomes for people, companies, and my community.

    I’ve been fortunate to do a great deal of that, solving business and socio-cultural problems over the past 14 years, alongside (mostly) wonderful people in diverse organizational environments.

    Creating better outcomes and solving problems is more important now than ever. I don't wish to change those aspects of my prior working years.

    But instead of focusing on human solutions, as a professional web developer, my aim will be to help build tools that create better outcomes - and thus "better futures" - on even larger scales by integrating my beloved coding savvy and relentless, proactive curiosity.

    Why Tech/Coding/Web Development?

    Over time, as I watched tech solutions grow more sophisticated, achieving efficiencies previously unthinkable, it became obvious that becoming the best, most well-rounded super-manager version of myself would require computer skills on par with those of business leadership.

    Likewise, some of my entrepreneurial aspirations seemed futile without the ability to forge my own tech tools. After exploring career paths in tech, the one I came to admire most (like so many others) was that of the developer, with one foot in science and the other in art.

    So I began learning to code - alone, online at first, then with help - and have been working and studying diligently since, with no stopping in sight. I have A LOT to learn yet, but from not owning a computer until I was a 19 year old undergrad, to devouring many self-learning resources (books, online, videos, etc.), to building apps as part of my JavaScript bootcamp curriculum, I also recognize that I've come a long way (again, no stopping in sight).

    *There are many more reasons I decided to pursue coding. To discuss further, or better yet, to share how you got started, send me a message.



    Looking Ahead

    I'm proud of my professional experience, but, without a doubt, my most outstanding achievements lie ahead. This makes me extremely excited for the future.

    I’m relatively new to Indy and even newer to the tech scene, but what I'd like both to know is this: I am record motivated and better poised than ever to make even greater, more meaningful contributions to a culture and legacy I'm proud to help shape, and mission(s) that leave me eager for more.
